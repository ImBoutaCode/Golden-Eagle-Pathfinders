About Pathfinders

Welcome to the Golden Eagle Pathfinder Club. We are pleased that you have chosen us to share your life with over the next year. Yes, that is right, the next year. While most clubs opt to shut down the week school lets out, we do not. We strongly believe that the summer months are when kids need the club the most. We plan to capitalize on the good weather and extra free time summer is so full of. We started this year long program in the 2012-2013 year. We are pleased to report that God has used the extra 3 months to bless our club with numerous life changing memories that we all will cherish the rest of our lives.


This year promises to be another great year for our club. Please view and or link to our Google calendar to get an overall feel for the activities planned this year. Please note that even the best made plans can be subject to change.


You have joined a youth club that is Christ centered and fun oriented. We strive to adhere to God’s Ten Commandments in all of our plans and activities. We will do our best to help you get to know Jesus. Our staff of adults and Teen Leaders in Training (TLT) are truly committed to your spiritual, social, mental, and physical well-being. Your physical and spiritual welfare is our number one priority. We desire to help you get to know the Lord through both study, hobbies and outdoor activities such as camping, hiking, backpacking, swimming, snowshoeing, bicycling, white water rafting, community outreach and various other life enhancing activities


With over 400 honors and 6 age appropriate Investiture Achievements, we will find new and exciting ways to enhance your journey that we call life. While we are not strict, we are firm in making sure you qualify for all awards or achievement certificates offered. As you pass from unit to unit in our organization, your efforts will have meaning and your achievements value.


You will enjoy our Drill and Marching exercises. You will be taught how to be a part of a team and how teamwork benefits the overall discipline of our club. The Golden Eagle Club has developed a reputation as “The Club to Challenge” at our Pathfinder Fair each year. We pride ourselves in being one of the best drill clubs in the Idaho Conference.


Our club has a history of forming a Bible Achievement Team that works together to study and memorize parts of the Bible. In the past few years our teams have successfully competed in regional, conference, union and division level tournaments. We look forward to seeing if you have what it takes to be on that team too. Hint: It’s all about effort and determination.

  • Friends recite the Pathfinder law and pledge, earn the Friend reading certificate, and much more. Friends are Pathfinders in or above 5th grade.

  • Companions recite the Pathfinder pledge and law, earn the Companion reading certificate, and much more. Companions are Pathfinders in or above 6th grade.

  • Explorers recite the Pathfinder pledge and law, illustrate through words and actions the Pathfinder pledge meaning, and earn the Explorer reading certificate, and much more. Explorers are Pathfinders in or above 7th grade.

  • Rangers recite the Pathfinder pledge and law, illustrate through words and actions the Pathfinder law meaning, and earn the Ranger reading certificate, and much more. Rangers are Pathfinders in or above 8th grade.

  • Voyagers recite the Adventist Youth (AY) aim, pledge, and motto, illustrate through words and actions the AY aim meaning, and earn the Voyager reading certificate, and much more. Additionally, Voyagers teach Camping Skills I, and Camping Skills II to other Pathfinders. Voyagers are Pathfinders in or above 9th grade.

  • Guides recite the Adventist Youth (AY) aim, pledge, and motto, illustrate through words and actions the AY aim meaning, and earn the Guide reading certificate, and much more. Additionally, Guides teach Knot Tying, Red Alert, Camping Skills III, and Camping Skills IV to other Pathfinders. Guides are Pathfinders in or above 10th grade.

  • Master Guide is the highest level of leadership within the Pathfinder program. It focuses on one’s personal spiritual life and growth first and foremost. General leadership skills are then woven into the sharpening of those skills, which are specifically geared to leading youth in God-ordained areas of development. Master Guides are Pathfinders 16+ years old and completed all requirements.


  • The Teen Leadership Training (TLT) program is designed to challenge and empower the teen Pathfinder with new and increased responsibilities, while allowing them the security of a place they know and love. The TLT experience will assist the Pathfinder teen to form adult-Pathfinder relationships as an expression of their developing identity as teenagers. The TLT member must learn how to give of themselves and how to receive from others. They must allow themselves to be known while getting to know others. TLTs are very special Pathfinders in 9th grade to 12th grade that want to give back to the club through leadership and aditional volunter work.

  • eXtreme Teen program is designed to challenge and empower the teen Pathfinder with new and exciting adventures. Some of the things these eXtreme Teens get to do include Sailing of the coast for 3 days, Rock climbing, deep woods camping and hiking, and whitewater adventures. eXtreme Teens are active Pathfinders age 13 to 19 that have a desire to go on and experience all new life enhancing events.
